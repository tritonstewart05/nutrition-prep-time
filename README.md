## Introduction

A common belief is that eating healthy — especially high-protein meals — takes more time than cooking regular meals. Because of this, many people avoid meal prepping since they think it is too time-consuming or inconvenient.

This project looks at whether that belief is actually true.

Using a dataset of 228,156 recipes, we explore whether meals that are higher in protein or more nutrition-focused actually take longer to prepare than other types of meals. This matters because time is one of the biggest barriers people mention when it comes to eating healthier or getting in shape. If healthy meals do not take much longer to make, it challenges the idea that meal prepping is too time-intensive and suggests that eating well may be more realistic than people think.
